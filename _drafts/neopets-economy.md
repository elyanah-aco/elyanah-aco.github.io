If you're at least as old as me, you've probably heard of [Neopets](https://www.neopets.com/) - 
that browser game where you own the eponymous pixel pets, play Flash games, and so on.

Unlike probably most of you who stopped playing it in the late 2000s to early 2010s, I actually played
it on-and-off throughout my teenage and young adult years. Even when I wasn't actively playing, I would 
still keep updated on news about the website. Now that there's a [Neopets revival](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2023/07/19/world-of-neopets-game-relaunch/) (not that it actually died), I thought it was prime time for me to go
back. 

While I could go on about many things about this site, I decided to write about the Neopets virtual economy because I actively participated
in it when I last played. I also took up some economics courses in college, so I at least have some sense of market dynamics.

Moving forward, this post only talks about the Neopoint (NP) economy; the Neocash economy, which involves real-life currency, is a completely different ballgame altogether.

## Definition of Terms
Before I continue, it would be nice to define some terms and abbreviations that I'll be using throughout this post.
* Dailies: Activities that can be done within Neopets on at least a daily frequency. Can provide free items, Neopoints, or both.
* [Item Database](https://items.jellyneo.net/) (IDB): Jellyneo's database of all Neopets items.
* [Jellyneo](https://www.jellyneo.net/) (JN): One of Neopets' recommended fan sites. Contains tons of guides and databases about the game.
* Neopoints (NP): The main virtual currency. Is mainly obtained by selling items, playing games, and participating in events. Used to purchase items and services within the website.



## Forces of Supply and Demand
How exactly does supply and demand work in the Neopets virtual economy? Note that the following discussion is not exhaustive, and is simply
based from my own observations while playing.

### How is supply determined?
All items are assigned a rarity that determines where and how often they are released. All items restocking in the in-game shops have rarities 
ranging from 1 to 99 (R1-R99), while non-restocking items have other rarities that indicate how they are obtained and whether or not they are tradable. For the purposes of this post, we focus on rarities 1-99, which are labeled as follows:

| Rarity     | Label       |
|------------|-------------|
| 1-74  | None | 
| 75-84 | Uncommon |
| 85-89 | Rare | 
| 90-94 | Very Rare | 
| 95-98 | Ultra Rare | 
| 99 | Super Rare |

Items can also be obtained through dailies, site events, or from buying from other users. As can be expected, items from dailies are more widely distributed among users, while only a specific subset of users would have access to items from events. We've both observed prices for rare items drop once they were given out as prices in dailies (or time-specific events with low participation required, like the Advent Calendar and the Neopies), and prices for high-participation site events increase through time as less users have them in circulation.

Aside from this, we also have to consider whether an item is one-time use or not. Food, books, album items and paintbrushes belong to the former category and are removed from circulation altogether once used. Toys, furniture, clothing and many others belong to the latter and can be reused and resold.

### How is demand determined?
Items required for specific achievements or are useful in some aspect of the website are more desirable and thus have higher demand than those that aren't. Album items like stamps and coins are among the most highly-priced and sought-after, since they're required for [stamp avatars](https://www.jellyneo.net/?go=avatars&id=stamps). Books and R90+ food count towards the [Neopian Book Award](https://www.neopets.com/book_award.phtml) and (Gourmet Food Club Award)[https://www.neopets.com/gourmet_club.phtml] respectively. Powerful Battledome weapons and training items like codestones are desired by Battledome players, and paintbrushes, petpets and clothing items are desired by customization enthusiasts.
